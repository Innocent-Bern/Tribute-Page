Kobe Bryant

The black mamba.

The following is a brief timeline of Kobe's life:

Early Life

Kobe Bryant was born on August 23, 1978 in Philadephia, the youngest of three children and the only son of former NBA player Joe Bryant and Pamela Cox Bryant. He started playing basketball when he was three, and the Lakers were his favorite team when he was growing up.

When he was six, his father retired from the NBA and moved the family to Italy to continue playing Professional basketball. Kobe became accustomed to the life in Italy and learnt to speak fluent Italian. He began playing basketball seriously while in Italy and his grandfather would mail him videos of NBA games for him to study.

During summers, he would come back to the United States to play in a basketball summer league. When Bryant was 13, the family moved back to Philadelphia, where he enrolled in eighth grade at Bala Cynwyd Middle School

NBA Draft

Kobe earned national recognition during his great high school career at Lower Merion High school. He played on the varsity team as a freshman. At the Adidas ABCD Camp, he earned the 1995 senior MVP award. Kobe ended his high school career as Southeastern Pennsylvania's all-time leading scorer at 2,883 points.

At the age of 17, Kobe decided to go directly into the NBA, becoming only the sixth player in NBA history to do so.

Bryant was drafted by the Charlotte Hornet as guard during the 1996 NBA draft. He was later traded to the Lakers. Since he was 17 his parents cosigned the deal with the Lakers. Bryant signed a three-year rookie contract totaling $3.5 million.

Professional Career

Kobe had a 20 year career as a professional basketball player with the Lakers. He was widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball players of all time. Bryant won five NBA championships, was an 18-time All-Star, a 15-time member of the All_NBA_Team, a 12-time member of the All-Defensive Team, the 2008 NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP), and a two-time NBA Finals MVP.

Bryant also led the NBA in scoring twice, and ranks fourth in league all-time regular season and postseason scoring. Kobe was the first guard in NBA history to play 20 seasons. His 18 All-Star designations are the second most all time, while it is the record for most consecutive appearances as a starter.

He gave himself the nickname "Black Mamba" in the mid-2000s, and the epithet became widely adopted by the general public.

Career Highlights

Throughout his career the Black Mamba broke numerous NBA records and had several awards and accomplishments. Below is a list of some of his records, awards and achievements:

1. 5-time NBA champion.
2. 2-time NBA finals MVP: 2009, 2010.
3. NBA MVP 2008.
4. NBA Slam Contest champion 1997.
5. 2nd most points in a game: 81 points
6. Most All-Star Game MVP awards won.
7. Most offensive rebounds in an All-Star Game.
8. Most points scored in a game in modern era basketball.
9. Youngest player to start a game.
10. Youngest player to win 3 championships.

Personal life

In November 1999, 21-year-old Bryant met 17-year-old Vanessa Laine. The two began dating and became engaged six months later in May 2000. They married on April 18, 2001, at St. Edward the Confessor Catholic Church in Dana Point, California.

The couple had four daughters: Natalia Diamante Bryant born in January 2003, Gianna Maria-Onore born in May 2006 (may her soul rest in peace), Bianka Bella Bryant born in December 2016 and Capri Kobe Bryant born June 2019.

Growing up Kobe was the youngest of three children. He had two sister Sharia and Shaya. Bryant was a practising Catholic and his family were regular attendees at Our Lady Queen of Angels Catholic Church in Newport Beach.
